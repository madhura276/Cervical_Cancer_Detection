Cervical Cancer Awareness

Cervical cancer is a type of cancer that occurs in the cells of the cervix, which is the lower part of the uterus that connects to the vagina. It is caused by certain strains of the human papillomavirus (HPV), which can be passed through sexual contact. Cervical cancer is preventable through vaccination and regular screening.

Key Facts

  • Cervical cancer is the 4th most common cancer in women worldwide.
  • Human papillomavirus (HPV) infection is the main cause of cervical cancer.
  • In most cases, cervical cancer develops slowly over several years.
  • Early detection through screening can greatly reduce the risk of developing cervical cancer.

Prevention and Screening

The most effective way to prevent cervical cancer is through vaccination against HPV. Regular screening with Pap smears or HPV testing can detect abnormal cell changes early, when they are easier to treat.

Note: Women are advised to start cervical cancer screening at the age of 21 and continue every 3 years until the age of 65, depending on the results of previous tests.

Symptoms of Cervical Cancer

In the early stages, cervical cancer may not cause any symptoms. As the disease progresses, the following symptoms may appear:

  • Abnormal vaginal bleeding (e.g., between periods, after sex, or after menopause)
  • Pain during intercourse
  • Pelvic pain or discomfort
  • Unusual vaginal discharge

Treatment Options

The treatment of cervical cancer depends on the stage of the cancer and may include surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, or a combination of these treatments. Early-stage cervical cancer has a high cure rate with appropriate treatment.

Important: If you experience any of the symptoms listed above, it is important to consult a healthcare professional for evaluation and possible testing.
